# Lagwan (kot) ‚Äî Grammar Reference

> Corpus-derived. All examples are attested verses. Learn the patterns from these; do not project Arabic, French, or Hausa grammar onto Lagwan.

## Word order

Lagwan is broadly SVO (Subject-Verb-Object). Temporal/scene-setting phrases front to clause-initial position. Determiners and relational particles follow the noun.

Typical clause pattern: `[Temporal frame] SUBJECT a VERB OBJECT PARTICLE`

Example: `Malwa a halak'a asama ka l…®ghwa…ó…®` ‚Äî GEN:1:1  
(God + PERF + created + sky + and + earth)

## Negation

Negation uses `sa` (negator) in combination with the verb. Pattern varies by tense/aspect:

- `sa a gh…®n X sa` ‚Äî does not do X
- `sa g…® X sa` ‚Äî (you) do not X (prohibitive)
- Example: GEN:2:17 `sa g…® z…®m gh…®li xu…óugwa h…®n s…®nel ngwa ha a bbi ka ngwa ha a m…ìawe ne sa`
- Example: GEN:20:4 `Abimalik a s…®na ga i sa p…®rt…®` ‚Äî Abimelech had not come near her

## Determiner 'ale'

`ale` is the standard post-nominal definite determiner (article), one of the top-5 most frequent words (4909 occurrences):

- `l…®ghwa…ó…® ale` ‚Äî the earth
- `Vushi ha Malwa ale` ‚Äî the Spirit of God
- `malika ale` ‚Äî the angel
- `am ale` ‚Äî the water
- `fuk'urayye hiya ni ale` ‚Äî his disciples (the disciples of him)

The density check `KOT-b…®la-ha-d…®ma-split` watches for `B…®la ha D…®ma` without following `ale` where the determiner is expected in formal register.
