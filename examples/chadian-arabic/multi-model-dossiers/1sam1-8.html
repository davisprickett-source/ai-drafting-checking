Decisions
  • Collapsed the four rhetorical questions to three (weep + eat combined) — matches how the human team reduces heavy question-stacks; the back-translation confirms no meaning was lost.
  • Used gaal ('he said') — Sonnet wrote gaalit ('she said'), which is wrong: Elkanah is male. A participant/agreement error the council caught.
  • raajil (j) not Sonnet's raagil (Egyptian g-for-jiim); ma not Sonnet's mish (Egyptian negation); achara not ʿashara (no initial ʿayin, ch not sh).
  • Orthography: pronoun suffixes attach WITHOUT a hyphen (galibki, raajilha, leeha, leeki) — the NT convention; the model drafts wrongly hyphenated.
  • Better = akheer/kheer (NT-attested ×65/×98), NOT the MSA borrowing ahsan (0 NT hits).
  • Grounded in attested NT phrases: maala tabki (John 20:15), galib haznaan (2 Cor 2:4), ma taakul (Rom 14:21).

Council
Strong convergence on the translation choice: both models collapsed the four questions to three. All the divergence was dialect hygiene — Sonnet leaked Egyptian/Sudanese forms (mish for negation, raagil with g-for-jiim) and a gender error (gaalit, 'she said', for the male Elkanah); the verifier flagged sh×3 + a marked initial ʿayin and suggested 'raajilha'. Opus stayed compliant (0 errors), so the proposed draft is Opus's.
Corpus QC
NT-corpus QC: 'ahsan' has 0 NT hits (MSA borrowing) → use akheer/kheer (×65/×98). Suffixes attach WITHOUT a hyphen: galibki/raajilha/leeki are the attested forms. 'achara'/'awlaad' acceptable. Sonnet's mish/raagil/gaalit/ʿashara rejected (dialect leaks + gender error).
Options
  • better: akheer / kheer — akheer (better) and kheer (good) are NT-attested ×65/×98; the MSA 'ahsan' has 0 NT hits — rejected.
  • questions: 3 (collapsed) / 4 (literal) — 3 reads natural (human-team practice); 4 is literal-faithful but heavy. Back-translation shows 3 keeps the full meaning.
  • ten sons: awlaad / wileed — awlaad = children (gender-neutral, BT flagged this); the Hebrew is specifically 'sons'.
Questions to decide

  • 'ten sons' — keep gender-neutral 'awlaad', or use a clearer 'sons' word? (the back-translation flagged the ambiguity)
