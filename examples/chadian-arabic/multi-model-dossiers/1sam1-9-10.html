note / decision
Why this draft
  • Used Opus's draft; Sonnet leaked sh (sharabu), kaan, and marked ʿayin (ʿala/ʿand/ʿatabit) — all rejected by the verifier.
  • 'drank' = charabo (ch, not sh) — the NT uses charab/tachrab; both models first wrote sharabu/shirbu.
  • 'ate' = akalo (not akalu) per the attested vowel.
  • Key terms from the retrieval: priest = raajil diin, temple = beet Allah.
  • 'doorpost' has no high-frequency Chadian word → rendered jamb baab ('by the door'); flag if more precision is wanted.
Options (your call)
  • seat: kursi / maqʼad — kursi is widespread/spoken; maqʼad reads MSA — prefer kursi.
  • doorpost: baab (door) / jamb al-ʼamuud (by the post) — Hebrew is specifically 'doorpost'; baab is the natural spoken referent, the post is more literal.
  • Shiloh: Chiiloh⟨?⟩ — place name; use ch (not sh) and max-2 vowels; confirm with the team.
Questions to decide

  • 'the priest' — raajil diin (construct) or raajil ad-diin (with article)?

1 Samuel 1:10
Hi nafsaha2 haznaana, wa saʼalat Allah2 wa gaaʼide tabki baki chadiid.
Hi nafsaha haznaana, wa saʼalat Allah wa gaaʼide tabki baki chadiid.
note / decision
Why this draft
  • Used Opus's draft — gaaʼide tabki / baki chadiid are attested (matt:2:18).
  • VERIFIED the prayer construction in the corpus (grep-corpus): petition = 'saʼal Allah' (direct object, ×9 — e.g. Elijah, jas:5:17) vs devotional 'salla le Allah' (with 'le' — phil:1:4 'nisalli le Allah'). Surfaced as a choice; Davis's 'le' instinct = the salla form.
  • VERIFIED soul/heart: nafs ×321 (the Hebrew nefesh cognate, but often 'self') vs galib ×51 (the dedicated emotion-heart). Both valid for 'bitter of soul' — surfaced.
  • 'bitter of soul' → the grief idiom haznaana, not a literal 'bitter' calque; Sonnet's 'marara' is unattested.
Options (your call)
  • pray: saʼalat Allah / sallat le Allah — saʼal Allah = petition (direct, ×9, cf. Elijah jas:5:17); salla le Allah = devotional prayer (le, phil:1:4). Hannah's earnest petition fits saʼal; the 'le' devotional form is also attested.
  • soul / heart: nafsaha / galibha — nafs is the Hebrew nefesh cognate and frequent (×321) but often means 'self'; galib (×51) is the dedicated emotion-heart — likely more natural here.
Questions to decide
  • Pray — petition 'saʼalat Allah' or devotional 'sallat le Allah'? (both attested; the 'le' form matches the salla verb)
  • 'bitter of soul' — nafs (soul, the Hebrew cognate) or galib (heart, the emotion-seat)?
