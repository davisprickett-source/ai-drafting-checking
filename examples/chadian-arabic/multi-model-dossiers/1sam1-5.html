1 Samuel 1:5
Wa laakin Alikhaana gaaʼid yantii le-Hanna nasiib akbar2, achaan yihibb Hanna, wa laakin Allah aagre-ha.?
Wa laakin Alikhaana gaaʼid yantii le-Hanna nasiib akbar, achaan yihibb Hanna, wa laakin Allah aagre-ha.
Decisions
  • Verb: chose gaaʼid (Opus) over Sonnet's kaan — kaan is a dialect leak. But the habitual 'used to give each year' aspect still needs a native call.
  • appayim: leaning 'double' (akbar) per French 'portion double'; left as an option.
  • Barren: aagre idiom — matches the French 'rendue stérile' and the back-translation; rejected the womb-calque.
  • Vocab: nasiib not Sonnet's unattested nassiib; rejected biyiddi (0 NT hits).
Source
Hebrew and-to-Hannah he-gave one portion APPAYIM (crux: 'double / two-fold' OR 'of two faces / worthy'); for he-loved Hannah, and-YHWH had-closed her-womb.
French Mais il donnait à Anne une portion double; car il aimait Anne, que l'Éternel avait rendue stérile.
Issue (1) appayim is a textual/lexical crux. (2) 'closed her womb' is a Hebrew idiom that must not be calqued.

Council
Verb aspect — Opus used gaaʼid, Sonnet used kaan. kaan is a flagged dialect leak (the verifier confirms 2× kaan). But gaaʼid marks ongoing-present, not habitual-past, so NEITHER is clearly right for 'he used to give each year'. This is the one genuinely open decision; needs a native call. appayim — French 'portion double' favours the 'double' reading (akbar / hissateen) over literal 'two'. Womb idiom — both models converge that aagre is the natural Chadian path; Sonnet's sakar rahimha is the formal source-faithful alternative. French 'rendue stérile' supports aagre.
Corpus QC
NT-corpus QC: 'give' = yanti (61×) / anta- / nazzal — Sonnet's 'biyiddi' has ZERO hits (not Chadian; Davis flagged this correctly). 'portion' = hissa (8×) or nasiib- (nasiibtah attested) — Sonnet's 'nassiib' (double-s) is unattested; Opus's 'nasiib' is closer. 'mithleen' also unattested. Opus's 'yantii' ≈ attested 'yanti'.
Options
  • barren — natural: … Allah khallaaha aagre — attested Chadian barren-idiom (Luke 1:7); matches French 'rendue stérile' AND the back-translation.
  • barren — formal: … Allah sakar rahimha — preserves the MT's active 'closed her womb' image (but 'sakar' for a womb is unverified Chadian).
  • appayim: hissa mithl hissateen / nasiib akbar — 'double portion' per LSG + the 'double' reading of appayim.
Questions to decide
  • How does Chadian render 'he used to give, every year' — gaaʼid + imperfect, a bare b-imperfect, or 'kal sana + verb'? (NOT kaan, and NOT biyiddi)
  • portion = hissa or nasiib? (hissa is better attested in the NT)
  • Is aagre usable causatively (khallaaha aagre = 'he made her barren') or only predicatively (hi aagre = 'she is barren')?
