Exegesis [E]discuss
Yoom waahid, Allah hajja le Yuunus wileed Amittaay wa gaal
issue The Hebrew is the prophetic-word formula וַיְהִי דְּבַז־יְהוָה ("the word of YHWH came to..."). The published text renders it with the plain verb 'hajja' (spoke/talked). This flattens a fixed prophetic-revelation formula into ordinary speech, losing the technical 'word of the LORD came' sense that frames Jonah as a prophet receiving a commission.
source Hebrew: wayəhî dəḇar-YHWH ’el-Yônâ — lit. 'the word of YHWH came/was to Jonah.' French (Segond): 'La parole de l'Éternel fut adressée à Jonas' — keeps the noun 'parole/word' as subject.
proposed
Yoom waahid, kalaam Allah ja le Yuunus wileed Amittaay wa gaal: Mirrors the Hebrew formula with 'kalaam Allah ja' (the word of Allah came), preserving the prophetic-revelation idiom.
Yoom waahid, Allah dassal kalaama le Yuunus wileed Amittaay wa gaal: Keeps Allah as agent but makes the divine 'word/message' explicit, closer to 'addressed his word to.'
Yoom waahid, Allah before-test... wassal kalaam le Yuunus wileed Amittaay wa gaal: Uses 'wassal kalaam' (delivered a word/message), foregrounding revelation over casual speech.
discuss Does Chadian Arabic need the 'kalaam Allah ja' formula to mark Jonah as a prophet receiving revelation, or is 'Allah hajja' an acceptable natural rendering for this audience? Is there an established formula already used elsewhere in the OT corpus for 'the word of the LORD came to X' that should be kept consistent here?
