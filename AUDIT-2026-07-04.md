# Methodology & Adoption Audit — 2026-07-04

Two adversarial passes over this system before sharing it with other teams: **(A)** a
methodology audit from the standpoint of a veteran SIL consultant (where would this
embarrass its builder?), and **(B)** an adoption walkthrough from the standpoint of a
team lead who has never talked to the builder. **(C)** lists every change made in
response; **(D)** lists what was deliberately left open and why.

Every claim below was verified against the actual files/tools this session (the linter
and scorer were run live on `multi-model/drafts/LUK23-draft.json`), not inferred.

---

## A. The SIL-consultant pass

### A1. The linter's silent failure modes

`tools/check-draft.ts` is regex rules + corpus-membership + density ratios. Its header
honestly says it doesn't judge meaning — but the *workflow language around it* invited
over-trust ("verifier is clean" as the gate to human review; a bare `✓ clean` output).
The error classes it can NEVER catch, and whether anything downstream does:

| Real translation error (CONNOT class) | Linter sees | Caught downstream by | Falls through end-to-end? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wrong participant — Pilate speaks instead of Herod; every word attested (Participant ref [D]) | nothing | back-translation diff; deep-tier participant lens | **Yes, if BT is contaminated or lens not run** — this was the single worst hole (see A3) |
| Dropped/added negation — negator is a high-frequency attested token; absence is invisible to token checks (Accuracy [Co]) | nothing | BT diff only | Same exposure as above |
| Tense/aspect flip between two *attested* markers (Tense & aspect [G]) | nothing (density checks only catch declared A-vs-B overuse) | BT partially; native reader | Often |
| Wrong sense of an attested word — the lexicon is membership, not sense (Lexical corr. [L]) | nothing | consultant-check lexical-grounding pass; native reader | Often |
| Wrong collocation of individually-attested words (Collocation [L]) | nothing | native reader only | Usually |
| Omitted clause — all remaining words attested (Omission [Co]) | nothing | BT diff (gloss-first BT makes this much harder to miss) | Reduced after this audit's BT fix |
| Attested vocabulary in wrong discourse order (`find-structures` is opt-in *retrieval*, not a check) (Discourse [D]) | nothing | drafter discipline in Step 2; native reader | Often |
| Illocutionary force skew — question rendered as statement (Illocutionary force [Co]) | nothing | native reader | Usually |
| Verse misalignment / boundary leakage | nothing | compare-drafts ref-keying, partially | Sometimes |

The one-line summary a consultant would give: **the linter certifies *words*, never
*reference or relation*. A fluent verse in which the wrong man dies comes out clean.**
That is fine as long as no signal anywhere implies otherwise — which is what A3 was about.

### A2. Council / relay false confidence

1. **Agreement is not independence.** All frontier models are trained on the same major
   translations and interlinears; their errors are correlated. The docs celebrated
   disagreement ("the disagreement IS the finding" — true) but never stated the
   converse: cross-model *agreement* is weak evidence, not verification. → fixed
   (`COUNCIL.md` new paragraph).
2. **The agreement metric is exact string match** (`score-draft.ts` `norm()` equality).
   Real verses almost never match exactly, so: (a) HIGH-via-agreement selects for
   trivial verses; (b) "models disagree" fires on any paraphrase — a noisy, low-precision
   signal. Left as-is mechanically (a similarity metric would add false comfort), but the
   tier-semantics footer now frames it honestly. Recorded in D.
3. **Back-translation contamination — the worst finding of the audit.** The BT check is
   the pipeline's ONLY accuracy net (see A1 table). `backtranslate-prompt.md` told the
   model "do NOT recall the original" *and then handed it the verse reference* —
   `Verse: <ref>` — to a model that has the Bible memorized in every major language. A
   model that recognizes the passage can back-translate from memory, silently restoring
   the very dropped clauses and wrong participants the check exists to catch, and the
   "BT matches source" line then reads as positive evidence of fidelity. An instruction
   not to recall does not remove the memory; you must remove the cue. → fixed: opaque
   segment ids (never refs), shuffled verse order, gloss-before-prose (a gloss forced to
   account for every token can't restore a dropped clause), "recognized passage" must be
   disclosed, suspiciously-perfect BT treated as a red flag. (`backtranslate-prompt.md`,
   `DRAFTING_PROTOCOL.md` Step 3 #6.)
4. **Deep-tier lens diversity.** `COUNCIL.md` suggests running lenses as Opus+Sonnet
   pairs — same vendor, correlated blind spots. Recorded in D (recommend seating the
   `independent` vendor in the adversarial-refuter lens at least).

### A3. Confidence signals that mislead

Verified live before fixing: scoring a **single** draft (no `--vs`) printed bare
`[HIGH]` on 37/56 verses of Luke 23 — with zero cross-model check, zero accuracy check —
while `check-draft` was flagging 84 warnings *on the same file* (the two tools never
talk to each other).

1. **HIGH was reachable from lexical attestation alone** (`agree === null` counted
   toward HIGH). A translator who trusts HIGH reads it as "this verse is right" and
   deprioritizes it — exactly backwards for a fluent-but-wrong verse. → fixed:
   single-draft scoring now caps at MEDIUM with the explicit reason
   `no cross-draft check (single draft caps at MEDIUM)`; HIGH now requires attestation
   ≥97% AND no markers AND a second draft agreeing.
2. **Every scorer run now ends with a semantics footer**: HIGH is a *mechanical* signal;
   a verse naming the wrong participant can score HIGH; accuracy is checked by BT +
   human, never by the scorer.
3. **`✓ clean` overspoke.** → now `✓ mechanically clean (orthography/lexicon only — …)`,
   and an all-clean summary explicitly lists what the linter cannot see.
4. **The self-report feedback loop (open, by nature unfixable mechanically):** fewer
   `⟨?⟩` markers → higher tier, and the loop's exit condition ("until … confidence is
   acceptable") rewards a model that under-flags. Recorded in D with the mitigation.

### A4. CONNOT coverage honesty

The taxonomy file mapped system checks onto CONNOT categories in a way a consultant
would read as a coverage claim; in reality ~2 of 9 category families are
deterministically detected, several more are model-detected with model-grade recall, and
most of Discourse/Rhetoric/Culture/[RL] is vocabulary only. → fixed: a "three grades of
covered" honesty note in `CONNOT-taxonomy.md` (deterministic / model-detected /
vocabulary-only), with the closing line "a category appearing in this file is a naming
convention, not a coverage claim."

### A5. Evidence rhetoric

The context-loading table was "tested, not assumed" on **n=1** (one verse, one model,
one language); the 7/10 fluent-speaker anecdote was presented as loop evidence without
its n. A consultant notices. → fixed: both now state their n and are framed as
directional/existence-proof, with "re-run on your own language."
(`DRAFTING_PROTOCOL.md`.) `MODES.md`'s benchmark already carried the right caveats.

## B. The team-lead adoption pass (START-HERE → SETUP → WORKFLOW)

1. **Unglossed jargon at first contact.** LWC, corpus, check-set, in-context pack,
   dossier — all assumed. → fixed: a 30-second glossary at the top of
   `FOR-TRANSLATION-TEAMS.md` (the advertised first read).
2. **"Tell me: Run the council" had no antecedent** — a newcomer doesn't know who "me"
   is. → fixed: `START-HERE.md` now says you're typing to the AI assistant in the
   Claude Code session.
3. **START-HERE broke the model-agnostic promise** — it hardcoded Opus/Sonnet/"your
   Gemini CLI" while COUNCIL/MODELS insist on roles. A reader with only a Gemini key
   reasonably concludes the tool isn't for them. → fixed: START-HERE and
   `dossier-template.md` now speak in `deep`/`workhorse`/`independent` roles.
4. **The two questions a team lead asks first — "where does my unpublished Scripture
   go?" and "what does this cost?" — were answered nowhere.** For a team in a
   sensitive region this is the close-the-tab item. → fixed: "Your data, cost, and
   safety" section in `SETUP.md`: L0 is fully local; L1+ sends prompt content to the
   vendors *you* configure, under their terms; `local` role for
   must-not-leave-the-building projects; the check-set is never transmitted; nothing
   spends money or writes to Paratext on its own.
5. **The steepest expertise cliff is `linter_rules[]` regex authoring** — SETUP step 1
   of "fill the language-specific config" casually assumes regex fluency the persona
   doesn't have, for the exact component the system's honesty depends on. → mitigated:
   `profiles/README.md` now ships a paste-ready "interview me, write my rules, then
   prove each rule fires correctly" prompt plus a warning about silent never-firing
   rules. (Full no-regex authoring flow recorded in D.)
6. **Residual Claude Code coupling** is real but honest: MODELS.md's `invoke` methods
   (cli/api/manual) give every role a non-subagent path. Recorded in D as a
   documentation-tone item, not a blocker.

**The one thing that, if missing, means they close the tab:** the data-safety answer
(#4). A team lead can forgive jargon and regex; they cannot forgive discovering
after the fact that draft Scripture went to a third party under unknown terms. It's why
that section now sits in SETUP before any AI tier is mentioned.

## D. Deliberately left open (with reasons)

1. **Scorer ⟷ linter integration.** `score-draft` still doesn't consume `check-draft`
   findings per verse (the linter is line-oriented, the scorer verse-keyed). Worth doing:
   emit linter findings as verse-keyed JSON and feed flag counts into the tier. Deferred —
   a real refactor, and the new footer removes the urgency.
2. **The ⟨?⟩ self-report loop.** A model that under-flags scores higher. No mechanical
   fix exists (it's self-report); the mitigation is cultural — the docs now frame tiers
   as triage-only — plus the cross-model and BT nets. A future `compare-drafts` signal
   ("model A flagged here, model B didn't") could partially externalize it.
3. **Exact-match agreement metric.** Low precision both directions; a similarity
   threshold would manufacture false HIGHs. Honest framing chosen over cleverness.
4. **Deep-tier lens vendor diversity.** Recommend: the adversarial-refuter lens should
   default to the `independent` vendor. One-line COUNCIL edit; left for a session where
   the lens prompts are being touched anyway.
5. **No-regex end-to-end profile authoring flow** (guided interview → rules → auto-test
   harness for rules). The paste-ready prompt covers the gap for now.
6. **`fetch-ot-reference.ts`** is referenced in START-HERE/WORKFLOW but absent from
   `tools/` — either restore it or de-reference it before sharing.
